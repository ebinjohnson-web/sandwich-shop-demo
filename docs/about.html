About the brand

A believable sandwich shop concept with a clean local feel.

This fictional brand is designed to feel real enough for a café, sandwich counter, or quick-service lunch shop in Ontario.

The idea

Warm, practical, and a little elevated.

Brick & Board is positioned between a casual lunch spot and a more polished café. The name suggests warmth, craft, and a grounded neighbourhood presence.

The website structure is kept clean so text can be changed later without fighting the layout.

What the brand stands for

Simple principles that fit a local shop.

01

Good lunch, fast

Fresh sandwiches without making customers wait longer than they should.

02

Clear choices

Less clutter, stronger item names, and easy paths for ordering and catering.

03

Neighbourhood warmth

A tone that feels friendly and local without looking dated or overly casual.

Visual direction

Rich food imagery, warm neutrals, and strong spacing.

The design uses oversized imagery, strong headings, rounded panels, and generous white space. That combination helps the site feel modern and premium without becoming too fancy.

Warm cream, brown, amber, and greens
Display serif for emphasis, clean sans serif for readability
Motion on scroll, soft parallax, and layered panels
